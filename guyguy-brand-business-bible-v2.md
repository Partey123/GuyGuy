## 1. The Name

**GuyGuy** is derived from the Ghanaian street expression "the guy" — referring to the trusted person who knows how to get things done, who has the connect, who you call when something needs fixing. In everyday Ghanaian conversation, "you need a guy for that" is how people find skilled help. GuyGuy takes that cultural shorthand and turns it into a platform. It is familiar, memorable, locally rooted, and carries an implicit promise — we have the right person for the job.

---

## 2. The Problem

Finding a reliable skilled tradesperson in Ghana is entirely word of mouth. There is no vetting, no price transparency, no accountability, and no protection when things go wrong. A homeowner who needs their ceiling fan fixed, their bathroom tiled, or their AC serviced has to ask a neighbour, post on a WhatsApp group, or rely on whoever their friend used last year. Sometimes the artisan shows up. Sometimes they do not. Sometimes the job is done well. Sometimes it is not. Either way, money changes hands on pure personal trust with no recourse.

On the other side, skilled artisans — electricians, plumbers, painters, welders, carpenters, AC technicians, phone repairers, makeup artists, photographers, tailors, home tutors — have inconsistent income, zero professional presence, and no way to grow beyond their immediate social circle. A brilliant electrician in Suame has no way to reach a client in Nhyiaeso who desperately needs him. The connection that should happen never does because there is no infrastructure to support it.

GuyGuy is that infrastructure.

## 3. Validation Notes

Before build began, conversations were held with artisans and potential clients to confirm the problem is real and the proposed solution is directionally correct. Key confirmations:

- Artisans in Suame Magazine report that 90%+ of their jobs come from personal referrals, with no platform, no professional profile, and no way to reach clients outside their immediate network
- Clients in Kumasi residential areas (Nhyiaeso, Airport Hills, Appiadu corridor) regularly use WhatsApp groups to find artisans and describe the experience as unreliable, inconsistent on pricing, and with no way to verify quality before hiring
- The escrow concept was received positively by artisans when framed as "the client's money is confirmed before you show up" — fear of not getting paid after completing a job is a specific pain point
- The profile-as-career-asset concept resonated strongly: multiple artisans expressed interest in having a shareable professional presence they could use on WhatsApp and Facebook

## 4. The Solution

GuyGuy is a two-sided marketplace that connects clients who need skilled services with verified artisans who provide them. It introduces verified identities, financial escrow, a reputation system that compounds over time, and a professional profile that artisans own and build over their career. It is mobile-first, MoMo-native, and built specifically for the Ghanaian market from day one.

---

## 5. Target Market

**Primary market:** Kumasi, Ghana — chosen as the launch city because it is Ghana's second largest city, home to Suame Magazine (one of the largest informal industrial clusters in West Africa), has a dense concentration of skilled artisans across every trade, and has an active smartphone-using middle class that regularly needs home and office services.

**Secondary markets (expansion phase):** Accra, Takoradi, Cape Coast, Tamale.

**Long-term vision:** West Africa — Nigeria, Ivory Coast, Senegal — markets with identical informal trade structures and the same trust gap between clients and artisans.

## 6. The Two Sides

### The Client
Homeowners, renters, office managers, small business owners — anyone who needs a skilled person for a job. They want reliability, fair pricing, and accountability. They are already searching for artisans on WhatsApp groups and Facebook. GuyGuy gives them a better, safer, faster way to find and hire.

### The Artisan

## 7. Brand Identity

### Tagline
**"The right guy. Every time."**

Secondary tagline for artisan-facing communication:
**"Your skills. Your profile. Your income."**

### Brand Personality
GuyGuy is confident, trustworthy, and local. It is not corporate. It does not feel like a foreign app adapted for Ghana. It speaks the language of the market — direct, warm, action-oriented. It respects both the client's time and the artisan's expertise. It treats artisans as professionals, not as gig workers.

Direct. No fluff. Warm but not over-familiar. Locally grounded without being exclusive. Speaks to both a student who needs their laptop fixed and a property manager who needs full office maintenance. The same brand voice works across both.

Modern, geometric, highly legible on mobile screens at all sizes.

Clean and neutral. Excellent legibility on small screens and low-resolution displays common on mid-range Android devices.

### Logo Direction
The GuyGuy wordmark uses the DM Sans Bold typeface with a custom ligature between the two Gs. The icon mark is a simplified wrench and lightning bolt merged — representing both physical trades and skilled technical work. The icon sits inside a rounded square, making it suitable as an app icon. Available in full color (navy on white), reversed (white on navy), and single color for stamps and merchandise.

### Artisan Profile Slug Format
Every artisan gets a public shareable URL at:
`guyguy.com.gh/a/[slug]`

The slug is generated automatically from the artisan's full name at profile creation. Example: Kwame Asante → `kwame-asante`. If that slug is already taken, a short suffix is appended: `kwame-asante-2`. Artisans cannot customise their slug in the MVP — this keeps generation deterministic and prevents squatting. Custom handles are a V2 feature.

### Dark Mode
Full dark mode support from launch. Background shifts to (#111827), card surfaces to (#1F2937), text to (#F9FAFB). Amber accent remains unchanged. Dark mode is not an afterthought — it is equally polished.

## 8. User Roles and Permissions

### Client
Creates an account with phone number and OTP. Browses artisans, makes bookings, pays through the app, confirms job completion, releases payment, raises disputes, leaves reviews. No subscription required. Free to use.

### Artisan
Creates an account, submits verification documents, sets up a profile with skills and location, adds their MoMo number for payouts, manages availability, receives and responds to bookings, chats with clients, tracks earnings, manages boost subscription, shares their profile link externally.

### Admin
Web-based panel (separate deployment at admin.guyguy.com.gh). Reviews and approves verification applications, manages disputes, monitors all transactions, views platform analytics, manages user accounts, handles flags and reports, pushes platform announcements.

## 9. The Verification System

Every artisan on GuyGuy goes through a verification process before their profile goes live. This is non-negotiable. It is the foundation of client trust.

**Verification requires:**
- Ghana Card or valid national ID
- Clear profile photo
- Skill category selection
- Years of experience declaration
- Service area definition (neighbourhood or district level)
- Phone number confirmed via OTP
- MoMo number for payouts (collected at this stage, not after first job)

Admin reviews submissions within 48 hours. Approved artisans receive a Verified badge on their profile. Rejected applications receive a reason and can resubmit.

Verification is free. It is not a revenue line — it is the trust infrastructure that makes everything else work.

## 10. The Boost and Badge System

This is GuyGuy's primary monetization from artisans and its primary differentiation from competitors.

### What a Boost Does
A boost increases an artisan's visibility in search results and category listings. Higher boost tier means the artisan appears higher in results when a client searches for their skill in their area. Without a boost, a verified artisan still appears in results — just below boosted artisans. The platform is never pay-to-play for basic access. Boost is an accelerator, not a gate.

### The Badge System
Badges are visual trust signals on every artisan profile. They communicate to clients how long an artisan has been committed to the platform. A client browsing profiles sees badges instantly — a Gold badge artisan has been investing here for a year. That is credibility that cannot be faked or purchased quickly.

**Badge Tiers:**

| Plan | Duration | Price | Badge Earned | Benefit |
|------|----------|-------|--------------|---------|
| Monthly Boost | 30 days | ₵25 | None | Improved search visibility |
| Bronze Plan | 3 months | ₵60 | 🥉 Bronze Badge | Sustained visibility + bronze badge on profile |
| Silver Plan | 6 months | ₵110 | 🥈 Silver Badge | Higher ranking + Recommended section placement |
| Gold Plan | 12 months | ₵200 | 🥇 Gold Badge | Top-tier ranking + Featured on home screen |

**Badge progression:** No Badge → Bronze (3 months) → Silver (6 months) → Gold (12 months). An artisan on the Monthly Boost has no badge. Their first badge is Bronze, which requires committing to 3 months. This is intentional — badges signal sustained investment, not one-month experiments.

### Why Artisans Will Pay
Because it directly affects their income. An artisan who earns ₵500 from one job through the platform will pay ₵25 to get more jobs. The boost cost is recoverable in a single booking. The decision is not "should I pay for software" — it is "should I invest in getting more clients." That is a question every artisan answers yes to.

### Subscription Psychology
₵25/month is airtime money. It does not feel like a subscription. It feels like recharging your business. The 3, 6, and 12 month plans offer discount incentives that make the longer commitment feel smart, not obligatory. Artisans on the Gold plan are invested in the platform. They will not leave because leaving means losing their badge, their rating, their job history, and their search visibility simultaneously.

## 11. The Payment and Escrow Model

### How It Works
Client books a service and pays the full quoted amount upfront through the app via MoMo or card. The money is held in escrow — it is not released to the artisan yet. The booking request is then sent to the artisan to accept or decline.

If the artisan **declines**: the full payment is refunded to the client immediately.

If the artisan **accepts**: the job is confirmed. The artisan shows up and does the work. The artisan marks the job complete in the app. The client receives a prompt to confirm completion. Client confirms → payment is released to the artisan's MoMo wallet within 24 hours. Platform deducts commission automatically before release.

### Why Escrow
Without escrow, clients fear paying before the job is done well. Artisans fear showing up and not getting paid. Escrow eliminates both fears simultaneously. The client knows their money is protected. The artisan knows confirmed money exists before they show up. Trust is not assumed — it is engineered into the transaction.

### Dispute Window
After the artisan marks a job complete, there is a 24-hour window for the client to raise a dispute. The client either confirms completion or raises a dispute within this window. If a dispute is raised, payment is frozen and admin reviews the case. Both parties can submit evidence — chat messages, photos, job description. Admin makes a ruling. Payment is released fully, partially, or refunded depending on the outcome.

If a client neither confirms completion nor raises a dispute within **72 hours of the artisan marking the job complete in the app**, payment is automatically released to the artisan. This prevents clients from using non-confirmation as leverage against artisans who have done the work.

Note: The 72-hour clock starts from **when the artisan marks the job complete**, not from the original scheduled booking date. These are different events.

### Commission Structure
**10% of the labour amount** on every completed transaction. Automatically deducted before artisan payout. No manual invoicing.

For **labour-only jobs**: commission is 10% of the total amount (which is the labour amount).

For **labour-and-materials jobs**: artisan declares a split in their quote — e.g. ₵200 labour + ₵150 materials = ₵350 total. Commission is 10% of ₵200 (the labour portion) = ₵20. The artisan receives ₵350 - ₵20 = ₵330. The commission never touches materials cost because GuyGuy played no role in sourcing materials.

This split is shown transparently on every quote so the client understands exactly what they are paying for.

## 12. The Artisan Profile as a Career Asset

This is the strategic lock-in mechanism that most marketplace platforms miss.

Every artisan on GuyGuy is building a career profile — not just listing a service. Their profile accumulates ratings, job completion count, portfolio photos, verified badge tier, and client reviews over time. This profile is theirs. It is shareable. An artisan can send their GuyGuy profile link in a WhatsApp conversation, on a business card, on their Facebook page.

"Check my GuyGuy profile" becomes a professional credential in the Ghanaian trades context where professional credentials barely exist. That is valuable to the artisan completely independent of platform bookings. It creates pull — artisans want the profile, they want it to look good, and they want the badge.

Leaving the platform means abandoning this asset. That is the real retention mechanism.

- GuyGuy Pro for Artisans — premium plan including priority support, advanced analytics, and automated client follow-up messaging

## 14. Launch Strategy

### Phase 1 — Supply Building (Months 1-3)
Go to the artisans. Physically. Visit Suame Magazine, Adum, Bantama, Asokwa. Talk to electricians, plumbers, welders, painters, and phone repairers. Explain the platform. Help them sign up and build their profile. Onboarding is completely free during this phase. No boost payments, no commission.

Collect artisan MoMo numbers during onboarding and register them as Paystack transfer recipients immediately — do not wait until the first payout. This ensures the payout pipe is ready before money needs to flow.

Target: 100 verified artisan profiles across at least 10 skill categories before a single client is onboarded.

**Parallel during Phase 1:** Submit Paystack business verification for transfer/disbursement permissions. This process takes 1-2 weeks in Ghana and is required before live MoMo payouts work. Do not leave this until Phase 3.

### Phase 2 — Demand Activation (Month 3-4)
Open to clients. Run targeted WhatsApp campaigns, Facebook and Instagram ads focused on Kumasi homeowners and renters. Partner with property management companies, estate developers, and corporate offices for bulk client acquisition — these are high-value accounts that book repeatedly and pay without friction.

Offer the first 200 client bookings commission-free to generate reviews and real job activity on the platform.

**GuyGuy for Business pilot (Phase 2 extension):** Identify 2-3 property management companies in Kumasi (Airport Hills, Nhyiaeso, Appiadu corridor). Offer them a dedicated support line and a simplified bulk booking flow. No new code needed — just a client account flag and personal outreach. These accounts will generate disproportionate transaction volume and referrals.

### Phase 3 — Monetization (Month 4-6)
Introduce boost subscriptions. Artisans who have already earned real income through the platform are now being asked to pay for more of what is already working. The conversation is easy — "you made ₵800 last month through GuyGuy, pay ₵25 to get more." Activate the 10% commission on all new transactions.

### Phase 4 — Expansion (Month 6-12)
Accra first. Then Takoradi. Same playbook each city — supply first, demand second, monetization third. Use Kumasi data (top skill categories, average job values, booking frequency) to target the right artisan types in each new city before arrival.

## 15. Competitive Positioning

No direct competitor exists in Ghana operating at this concept. The closest are:

**Facebook groups and WhatsApp groups** — unstructured, no vetting, no payment protection, no reviews. GuyGuy wins on trust and accountability.

**Tonaton** — classified listings, not a service marketplace. No booking flow, no escrow, no artisan profiles. GuyGuy wins on the entire experience layer.

**Bolt Food / Glovo** — delivery platforms, not relevant. Different category.

**International platforms (Thumbtack, TaskRabbit)** — do not operate in Ghana. No MoMo integration. No understanding of local market. GuyGuy wins by simply existing and being local.

The competitive moat is not technology. It is trust infrastructure built specifically for the Ghanaian market, the artisan profile as a career asset, and the badge system that makes verification and platform investment visible to clients.

## 17. The Long-term Vision

GuyGuy starts as a booking platform. It becomes the professional identity layer for Ghana's informal skilled workforce. Every artisan in Ghana has a GuyGuy profile the way every professional in the West has a LinkedIn. Every client in Ghana searches GuyGuy the way every Western consumer searches Google for local services.

The platform eventually becomes the bridge between informal artisans and formal economic participation — providing income data that supports microfinance applications, skills certification that supports professional development, and a client base that supports consistent livelihood. The artisan who joins GuyGuy as an electrician with no professional presence leaves with a verified career history, a star rating built over hundreds of jobs, a portfolio of completed work, and a financial track record that banks can read.

That is the real product. Not an app. An economic elevator for Ghana's skilled workforce.
